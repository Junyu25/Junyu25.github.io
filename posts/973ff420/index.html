I’m a big fan of Scoot H. Young’s Blog. I was fascinated by his MIT CHALLENGE. Ultralearning is always one of my ultimate goal. And now I think this is time for me to march out.

The CHALLENGE

The first challenge of mine is focus on bioinformatics. What I major is bioengineering, but I think I’m really good at Computer Science. So to made the best of my genius, and be more competitive, I think the bioinformatics is the best subject for me to learn at the limited time.

After that, I found nearly the best course for me to learn in Coursera. It’s the Bioinformatics Specialization held by the University of San Diego. It contain 7 courses.

I have tried the firt course and fascinated by the hight quality materials. It’s contain less video courses than I think, most of the time I spent on is a thirt part tool called stepik. The best part is there are few code challenges in every sections. It’s easy for me to understand the bio process, but when it comes to the theory of algrithm, I always suck in there for long time and need to search a lot of information. I think it quite challenging for me to code, expecially I need to understand the material in second language. Howerver the outcome could we really good. So I decide to take this course and get the certificate whin 3 month, which is needed 3 hours per week and approximatly 12 months to complete. I will update the process and upload the note I make.

The Strategies

When doing this challenge, I will use Jupyter Notebook to take the note and run the code.
The additional material is the book writtent by the instructors, which is called Bioinformatics Algorithms: an Active Learning Approach.
